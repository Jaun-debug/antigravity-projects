<h2>The Cost of Travel to Namibia</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before you even step foot on Namibian soil, there are a few initial expenses you’ll need to consider. The most significant ones? Flights and visas.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Windhoek, the capital city, serves as a central starting point for many travelers exploring Namibia.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3>Flight Costs from Europe to Namibia</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Flights from Europe to Namibia are one of the first major expenses to keep in mind. Depending on your departure city, you can expect to pay anywhere between €600 and €1,200 for a return flight. Direct flights to Namibia are limited, but you’ll find connections through major hubs like Frankfurt, London, and Amsterdam.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If you're looking for the best deals, aim to book your flight at least three to six months in advance. The earlier you book, the better the chances of snagging a lower price. Consider traveling during the low season (May to September) when the prices are typically lower, and the weather is great for safaris.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3>Visa Costs for Namibia</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Most European passport holders do not need a visa for stays of up to 90 days in Namibia. If you’re staying longer, however, you’ll need to apply for a visa. The good news? This process is relatively straightforward and inexpensive. Expect to pay €30 to €50 for a short-term tourist visa.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s always a good idea to double-check with your local embassy or consulate, just in case there are changes to visa policies closer to your departure date.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2>Currency and Payment Tips: Namibian Dollar</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One thing that often catches travelers off guard is the currency. Namibia uses the Namibian Dollar (NAD), though the South African Rand (ZAR) is also accepted. Both currencies are pegged to the South African Rand, so you won’t have to worry too much about exchange rates.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul>
<li>
<p>Exchange Rates: You’ll find exchange rates around €1 = NAD 20. If you're using euros, you can exchange your currency at the airport, banks, or exchange bureaus. Be sure to shop around for the best rates!</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>ATMs and Cards: Credit cards are widely accepted in cities and tourist hotspots, but cash is king in more remote areas. ATMs are available in major towns, but don’t rely on them in national parks or rural areas. It’s a good idea to carry enough cash for emergencies and rural expenses.</p>
</li>
</ul>
