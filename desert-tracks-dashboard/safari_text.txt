A self-drive safari gives you the freedom to explore Africa’s wild spaces at your own pace. You travel in your own rental vehicle, stop for photos whenever you like, and enjoy the adventure of the open road with nature as your guide. Do I need a 4x4 vehicle? For most routes, a 4x4 is recommended to handle gravel roads and sandy tracks comfortably. It also gives you access to more remote parks and viewpoints. How long should I plan for my safari? We suggest planning between&nbsp;10 – 16 days&nbsp;to enjoy the journey without feeling rushed. This allows time for game drives, scenic stops, and relaxing at your lodges or camps. Is it safe to drive on safari? Yes, absolutely — as long as you follow simple guidelines. Keep to marked roads, respect speed limits, and avoid driving after dark. Wildlife and road safety are top priorities, and most routes are well-maintained and clearly signposted. What documents or permits do I need? You’ll need a&nbsp;valid passport,&nbsp;driver’s licence&nbsp;(an international one if you’re visiting from abroad),&nbsp;visa&nbsp;if required,&nbsp;vehicle and travel insurance, and for families,&nbsp;children’s unabridged birth certificates. We’ll help make sure everything is in order before your journey. What should I pack or have in the car? A few essentials include water, a first-aid kit, a spare tyre, sun protection, and a good map or GPS. Most rental vehicles come fully equipped for safari travel. We will also suggest some other useful tips before arrival. What are the main driving rules in national parks? Stay on designated roads, keep a safe distance from wildlife, and never exit your vehicle unless in an approved area. Respect park gate times and always follow ranger instructions. Will I have mobile or GPS signal everywhere? Coverage can be limited in remote areas, but offline maps and preloaded routes work perfectly. We provide reliable navigation tools for peace of mind. How much does a self-drive safari cost? Costs depend on your route and accommodation style. Self-drive safaris are flexible — you control your budget and pace. We’ll help design an itinerary that suits your travel style. Can I cross borders during my trip? Yes, many travellers drive across borders into neighbouring countries. Just let us know in advance so we can arrange the necessary vehicle and insurance documents. What happens if I get a flat tyre or a mechanical issue? Our support team and partner networks are always on call. Most rental vehicles have spares and basic tools, and roadside assistance is just a phone call away. Can I drive at night? It’s best to plan drives during daylight to enjoy the scenery and ensure visibility. Park gates usually close at sunset — perfect timing to relax and watch the stars. Is THIS safari suitable for families or first-timers? Definitely. With clear roads, well-marked routes, and welcoming lodges, self-drives are a wonderful family adventure. We’ll tailor the itinerary to suit your comfort level. Do I need special insurance? Comprehensive insurance is always included with our rentals. We’ll make sure you understand exactly what’s covered before you start your journey.
