Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Windhoek — Ti Melen Boutique Guesthouse

Day 1

Your Namibia safari begins in Windhoek, a relaxed yet cosmopolitan gateway to Southern Africa. After landing at Hosea Kutako International Airport, you collect your vehicle and settle into your boutique accommodation.

The afternoon is yours to ease into the journey—explore charming cafés, enjoy premium dining, or simply unwind in a tranquil garden setting. The blend of German heritage, African culture, and modern comforts sets the tone for your luxury Namibia self-drive safari.

02
Sossusvlei / Namib Desert — Le Mirage Resort & Spa

Day 2–4

Your journey continues into the iconic Namib-Naukluft National Park via the dramatic Spreetshoogte Pass—one of Namibia’s most scenic drives with panoramic desert vistas.

Arriving near Sossusvlei, you enter a world of towering red dunes, endless horizons, and striking contrasts. Early mornings are dedicated to exploring Deadvlei’s ancient camelthorn trees and climbing dunes like Big Daddy for sunrise views.

Afternoons invite relaxation at your lodge or optional high-end experiences such as hot air balloon safaris over the Namib Desert, followed by champagne breakfasts. This is Namibia’s most photographed region—raw, dramatic, and unforgettable.

03
Swakopmund & Skeleton Coast — Swakopmund Luxury Suites

Day 4–6

Traveling via Solitaire and the Gaub and Kuiseb canyons, the landscape shifts from desert to ocean as you arrive in Swakopmund—a coastal highlight of any Namibia itinerary.

This seaside town blends colonial charm with adventure travel. Days can be tailored with premium activities such as Sandwich Harbour 4x4 excursions, marine cruises, kayaking, or scenic flights over the Skeleton Coast.

Evenings are reserved for fine dining and a relaxed coastal atmosphere, making Swakopmund a perfect balance of adventure and comfort.

04
Twyfelfontein / Damaraland — Twyfelfontein Adventure Camp

Day 6–7

Heading north along the Skeleton Coast, you visit Cape Cross, home to one of the largest Cape fur seal colonies in the world. The journey continues inland to Twyfelfontein in Damaraland.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers a cultural and geological experience, with ancient San rock engravings, desert-adapted wildlife, and dramatic landscapes. Optional nature drives along the Huab River may reveal desert elephants in their natural habitat.

The region offers a remote, authentic Namibia safari experience with a strong sense of history and place.

05
Etosha National Park — Okutala Etosha Lodge

Day 7–9

Your safari experience deepens as you enter Etosha National Park, one of Africa’s premier wildlife destinations.

Game drives focus around waterholes where wildlife gathers—elephant, lion, rhino, giraffe, and large herds of plains game. The vast Etosha Pan creates a unique backdrop, especially during golden hours.

Whether self-driving or joining guided game drives, this region delivers exceptional wildlife viewing and classic African safari moments.

06
Waterberg Plateau — Waterberg Plateau Lodge

Day 9–10

Your journey continues to the lush and elevated Waterberg Plateau National Park, a striking contrast to the arid landscapes before.

Here, sandstone cliffs, rich vegetation, and diverse wildlife create a more secluded safari experience. Activities include guided nature walks, rhino tracking, and scenic plateau hikes with sweeping views across northern Namibia.

This destination offers a quieter, more exclusive end to your safari adventure.

Windhoek — Hosea Kutako Airport

Day 11 — Return to Windhoek

After breakfast, you journey back toward Windhoek, reflecting on a diverse Namibia safari filled with desert landscapes, coastal adventures, cultural encounters, and exceptional wildlife.

Vehicle drop-off at the airport concludes your seamless self-drive safari through Namibia—an experience defined by freedom, variety, and unforgettable moments.
