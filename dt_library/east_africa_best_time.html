Best Time to Visit East Africa

East Africa offers spectacular year-round wildlife viewing, but timing your visit correctly can enhance your experience, particularly if you wish to witness the Great Migration.

The Long Dry Season

June to October

This is widely considered the best time for a classic safari. The vegetation is sparse, making wildlife easier to spot as animals gather around rivers and waterholes. The weather is generally sunny and dry. This period also coincides with the dramatic river crossings of the Great Migration in the northern Serengeti and Masai Mara (typically July to September).

The Calving Season & Short Rains

January to March

Following the short rains of November and December, the plains turn lush and green. From late January to March, the wildebeest migration gathers in the southern Serengeti for the calving season. Over 500,000 calves are born during this time, attracting high concentrations of predators. It’s an action-packed period with fewer crowds than the peak dry season.
