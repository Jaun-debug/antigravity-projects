Frequently Asked Questions About East Africa Safaris

What makes East Africa unique for safaris?

East Africa is home to the Great Migration, one of the most spectacular wildlife events on Earth, moving between Serengeti National Park and Maasai Mara.

When is the best time to visit?

Wildlife viewing is excellent year-round, but peak migration periods are typically June to October in Kenya and December to March in Tanzania.

Can I combine safari with a beach holiday?

Yes, combining safari with a stay in Zanzibar is one of the most popular itineraries, offering a perfect bush-and-beach experience.

What type of safaris are available?

Options include luxury lodge safaris, mobile tented camps, fly-in safaris, and guided overland journeys.

What wildlife can I expect to see?

Expect the Big Five — lion, leopard, elephant, rhino, and buffalo — as well as vast herds of wildebeest, zebras, and abundant predators.

Is East Africa suitable for first-time safari travellers?

Absolutely. It’s one of the best regions for first-time safaris due to its accessibility and high wildlife density.

How do I travel between destinations?

Travel is typically by light aircraft between parks, especially when combining Kenya, Tanzania, and Zanzibar.
