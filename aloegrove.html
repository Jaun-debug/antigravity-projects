Our Philosophy

Putting You
the Guest First

At Alöe Grove, we pride ourselves in putting you — the guest — first. From the moment you arrive on our private airstrip to the moment you reluctantly leave, every detail is attended to with genuine Namibian warmth.

We are a small, private family-run lodge. We keep our groups small — a maximum of 24 guests at any one time — so that every guest receives personal attention and every experience feels exclusive.

Our 7 en-suite bungalows each feature private balconies with an uninterrupted 360° view over the Namibian savanna and the magnificent Waterberg Plateau. There is nowhere quite like it.

The Natural World

Environment

Our Ecosystem

The Land of Alöe Literalis

The name Alöe Grove derives from the Aloe Literalis — a remarkable succulent species that grows abundantly across the property, adapted over thousands of years to thrive in the harsh but beautiful Namibian climate.

Our farm covers 8,300 hectares of diverse savanna habitat, bordering the renowned Waterberg Plateau — one of Namibia's most important conservation areas. The plateau's sandstone cliffs, rising dramatically above the surrounding plains, create a stunning backdrop and a rich refuge for wildlife.

The property encompasses a variety of ecosystems — from open grass plains and acacia thornbush to seasonal pans and rocky outcrops — each supporting a unique community of plants and animals.

Flora & Fauna

A Living Wilderness

The 8,300 hectares support an extraordinary diversity of wildlife. Herbivores such as eland, kudu, gemsbock (oryx), giraffe, zebra, wildebeest, and warthog roam freely across the property. Predators including lion, leopard, and cheetah are resident on the farm.

The birdlife is equally remarkable. The Waterberg biome is one of southern Africa's most significant birding destinations, with over 200 species recorded including raptors, ground birds, and migratory species.

The aloe plants themselves are a vital resource — providing both food and shelter for insects, birds, and small mammals throughout the year.

"We are custodians of this land. Our commitment is to conservation, sustainability, and sharing this extraordinary environment with guests who appreciate its fragility and beauty."
— Alöe Grove Lodge
